Dataset information

NSL-KDD intrusion detection benchmark

The study uses NSL-KDD train and test files with binary-label ARFF variants, attack-type TXT variants, and reduced subsets for smaller experiments.

Supplied files

Dataset package map

KDDTrain+.ARFF

Full train set with binary labels in ARFF format.

KDDTrain+.TXT

Full train set including attack-type labels and difficulty level in CSV format.

KDDTrain+_20Percent.ARFF

20 percent subset of the full ARFF training set.

KDDTrain+_20Percent.TXT

20 percent subset of the full TXT training set.

KDDTest+.ARFF

Full test set with binary labels in ARFF format.

KDDTest+.TXT

Full test set including attack-type labels and difficulty level in CSV format.

KDDTest-21.ARFF

Subset excluding records with difficulty level 21 out of 21.

KDDTest-21.TXT

TXT subset excluding records with difficulty level 21 out of 21.

Why NSL-KDD

Improvements over KDD'99

NSL-KDD reduces duplicate and redundant records, giving classifiers a fairer evaluation surface and making full-set experiments practical for comparative research.

No redundant train records

Classifiers are less biased toward frequent duplicated records.

No duplicate test records

Methods are not rewarded simply for detecting repeated samples well.

Balanced difficulty sampling

Selected records vary by difficulty level, widening performance differences between learning techniques.

Reasonable experiment size

Complete train and test experiments are affordable, improving consistency across research works.

Attack taxonomy

Five-class target mapping

DoS

Denial-of-service traffic such as neptune, smurf, back, land, pod, teardrop, apache2, mailbomb, processtable, udpstorm, and worm.

Normal

Benign network connections that do not belong to an attack signature group.

Probe

Reconnaissance activity such as ipsweep, mscan, nmap, portsweep, saint, and satan.

R2L

Remote-to-local attacks such as guess_passwd, ftp_write, imap, phf, multihop, warezmaster, snmpguess, and xlock.

U2R

User-to-root attempts such as buffer_overflow, loadmodule, perl, rootkit, sqlattack, ps, and xterm.
